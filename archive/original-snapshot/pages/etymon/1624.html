#1624 PTB *la MALE

This etymon is very well attested in the NE Indian Areal Group, Lolo-Burmese, and Jingpho, with possible cognates also in Himalayish. STC (p. 96) recognizes a ‘masculine suffix’ *‑la, “used with words for animals (in Tsangla, Digaro, Nung, Kachin, Burmese-Lolo, Konyak, Garo-Bodo, Mikir, and Meithei)”.

Strangely enough, there is a homophonous root #5493 PTB *la(ː) FEMALE / DAUGHTER with the antonymous meaning FEMALE / DAUGHTER. Cf. Benedict 1981, 1991.
