This etymon is a prime example of the alternation between homorganic stop and nasal. Most subgroups reflect the *nasal, while Lolo-Burmese attests to the *stop.

Galo seems to have an interesting doublet sɯ ⪤ `hɨɨ. Note also the Trung [Dulong] doublet śiŋ ⪤ śik.

LTSR 118(a-b) also reconstructs two allofams, with the nasal-final variant attested in Bisu tsɯ̀ŋ.

As suggested in DL p. 1221, the poetic Lahu form šɔ̂ might be from the nasal-final variant, although it is tonally aberrant.

The Chepang forms with final ‑ŋʔ suggest a stage intermediate between a final nasal and a final stop.
