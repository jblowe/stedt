#201 PTB *m-kal ⪤ *s-gal BACK (lower) / KIDNEY / LOAD / CARRY
Reconstructed mesoroots below:
  • PKC *kal KIDNEY
  • PTk *kal KIDNEY

The PKC etymon *khel (a meso-root of #202 PTB *(k/g)yal BACK (lower) / BUTTOCKS / HIPS) definitely seems related to this root, perhaps pointing to a PKC variant *k(y)al.

The meaning of this etymon ranges from KIDNEY / SMALL OF BACK to the notion of CARRY ON THE BACK / LOAD. There also seems to be a relationship with the widespread root #2355 PTB *m‑kul TWENTY / ALL, which perhaps indicates an original meaning like ‘a complete load; everything that can be placed on a beast of burden at one time.’

The original meaning of the graph 荷 of the Chinese comparandum was ‘lotus’, as witness its “grass radical”, but it came to be used as a loan character for the concept CARRY (in the Confucian Analects [Lún Yǔ 论语]) and for SUSTAIN (in the Zuǒ Zhuàn [左傳], one of the commentaries on the Spring and Autumn Annals).
