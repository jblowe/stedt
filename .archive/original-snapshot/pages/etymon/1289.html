#1289 PTB *g-li-k ARMPIT / TICKLE
Reconstructed mesoroots below:
  • PCN *k-lik TICKLE
  • PTk *rik ARMPIT

This “funny” root is quite well attested in several branches of the family, usually as a sesqui- or di-syllable. Several languages (Ao, Bisu, Magar) show a variant with final velar stop, while others (WB, Gurung) show some sort of laryngeal feature after the vowel.

Yang 1985 cites several extra-TB lookalikes: Khmer kliək, Indonesian ketiak ‘armpit’, Cham kəlĕk ‘tickle’. See also Benedict 1975a:410 (“Austro-Thai Language and Culture, with a glossary of roots”).
