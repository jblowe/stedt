#803 PTB *s-na ⪤ *s-naːr NOSE
Reconstructed mesoroots below:
  • PKC *hnaar NOSE
  • PCN *na(ʔ) NOSE
  • PTk *na NOSE
  • TGTM *ᴬʰna NOSE

Chantyal nak/nakh is a borrowing from Nepali nāk, but the superficially similar Tiddim form naːk³ is a genuine descendant of PKC *hnaar. (The development of final ‑r to a velar is regular in Tiddim.)

A few Tani languages (Idu, Kaman) have developed secondary final consonants (‑m, ‑ŋ)). There are separate but perhaps related roots: #1415 PTB *m/s‑nam SMELL, #805 PTB *s‑nap SNOT / NASAL MUCUS. Cf. all the English words relating to the nose that begin with the clusters sm- or sn-.

Noteworthy is the Qiang (Mawo) reflex with initial st‑ rather than sn‑. This obstruentization of n‑ after the s‑ prefix had hitherto been noted only in Kanauri and Chinese. See HPTB:103.
